# Assessment Rubric

Use this rubric to keep `english-study` sessions consistent.

## Level Signals

- `beginner`: struggles with basic sentence building, high-frequency vocabulary, or direct meaning transfer in both directions
- `lower-intermediate`: can express simple daily meaning but makes frequent grammar or word-choice errors
- `intermediate`: usually communicates intended meaning, but accuracy and naturalness break under longer or less familiar prompts
- `upper-intermediate`: handles nuance and longer structures with occasional unnatural phrasing or precision gaps
- `advanced`: consistently accurate, natural, and context-aware across both directions

## Evidence Categories

Judge every answer on these dimensions:

- `grammar`: tense, articles, agreement, word order, particles, sentence structure
- `vocabulary`: correctness, range, collocation, precision
- `context`: naturalness, situational fit, implied meaning, tone
- `culture`: politeness, convention, idiom, common real-world usage

## Difficulty Adjustment

- move up slightly after a mostly correct answer with only minor issues
- hold steady after mixed performance
- move down slightly after a clear breakdown in comprehension or production
- do not make large jumps based on one answer

## Stop Rule For Placement

Stop when:

- both translation directions have been sampled multiple times
- the learner has seen at least a small spread of difficulties
- the current level estimate is supported by repeated evidence
